Our Story

Why we built a remote observatory 4,000 metres above sea level – and why you’re the reason it exists.

From a Polluted Sky to a Window on the Universe

I still remember the first time I pointed my tiny refractor at the Orion Nebula from my rooftop in the city. I saw a faint, grey smudge – nothing like the vivid photographs I’d admired online. The culprit? Light pollution. That was the moment I realised that if you can’t escape the city, the universe stays hidden.

So I did something about it. I travelled to Spiti Valley, a cold desert in the Himalayas where the sky is classified Bortle 1 – the darkest on the planet. There, I set up a fully automated observatory with professional-grade equipment. But this isn’t just my private playground. I opened it to the world.

Why RemoteScope Exists

Democratising Deep‑Sky

Not everyone can drive 12 hours into the mountains or spend ₹3 lakhs on a Takahashi refractor. We’ve removed both barriers. All you need is a browser and curiosity.

Learn by Doing

Point the telescope yourself, choose filters, and see the results live. It’s the fastest way to understand how astrophotography works – no expensive trial and error.

Community & Discovery

Capture data for science, share your images with our growing community, or collaborate with other enthusiasts across the globe – all through a single platform.

Sustainable Astronomy

Instead of everyone buying their own equipment that sits idle 90% of the time, we share one world‑class rig efficiently. Less waste, more stars.

Inspiring the Next Generation

Students, hobbyists, and dreamers who never imagined touching a telescope can now operate cutting‑edge hardware – and we love seeing their faces light up.

The Gear That Makes It Possible

Every piece of equipment was chosen to deliver publication‑quality images – even from your browser.

Takahashi FSQ‑106ED

A legendary apochromatic refractor renowned for pinpoint stars across a wide field – the same telescope used by observatories worldwide.

ASI2600MC Pro

A cooled, 26‑megapixel colour camera that captures stunning detail on nebulae, galaxies, and comets without the complexity of monochrome sensors.

10Micron GM2000 HPS

Absolute‑encoder robotic mount that tracks the sky with sub‑arcsecond precision – no guiding needed in most conditions.

ASIAIR Plus

The brain of the observatory. It lets you control everything – focusing, plate‑solving, sequencing – from anywhere in the world, in real time.
