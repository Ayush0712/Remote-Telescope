From a Grey Smudge to a Bortle‑1 Observatory

I still remember pointing my first refractor at Orion from my rooftop in the city. All I saw was a faint, grey smudge—nothing like the vivid images I’d admired online. Light pollution had stolen the universe. That night I decided to do something about it.

I travelled to Spiti Valley and set up a fully automated observatory. But this was never meant to be just my private playground. I opened it to the world because I believe everyone—regardless of where they live or how much they can spend—deserves a front‑row seat to the cosmos.
